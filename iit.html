What is NEET?

NEET stands for National Eligibility cum Entrance Test. It is a single entrance exam for students who wish to pursue undergraduate medical (MBBS), dental (BDS) and AYUSH (BAMS, BUMS, BHMS, etc.) in government/private medical or dental colleges in India. The conducting authority of NEET UG is the National Testing Agency (NTA).

Admissions to AIIMS

The All India Institutes of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) is a group of autonomous government public medical universities of higher education. At present, there are 23 AIIMS campuses in India out of which 19 are functioning and the rest are under construction. Till 2019, medical aspirants who had dreamed of joining one of the AIIMS institutes were required to clear the AIIMS MBBS examination. From the academic year 2020, admission to undergraduate medical courses in AIIMS are given on the basis of NEET score.

Admissions to JIPMER

The Jawaharlal Institute of Postgraduate Medical Education & Research (JIPMER), funded by Indian central government, is a premier medical college of India. JIPMER was first established on 1 January 1823, and it is the oldest institute teaching European medicine in Asia. Admission for the MBBS program at JIPMER, Karaikal & JIPMER, Puducherry will be given on the basis of NEET score.
